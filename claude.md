### Core Theme
The book measures the **dual impact of ICT** (positive AND negative) in achieving UN SDGs in developing countries.

**Positive impacts covered by the book:**
- Energy savings due to ICT (smart grids, teleworking)
- CO2 reduction via smart logistics
- Efficiency gains in resource management (precision agriculture, waste)
- Digital inclusion improvements
- Better health/education outcomes via e-health, e-education
- Transparent e-government services

**Negative impacts covered by the book:**
- High energy consumption of data centers and devices
- GHG emissions
- E-waste generation
- Use of non-renewable/hazardous materials
- High water consumption in manufacturing
- Data privacy and security incidents

## Writing Style Guidelines

Based on author's prior Springer publication (Wireless Networks, 2019, DOI: 10.1007/s11276-017-1630-y):

- **First person plural:** "We suggest", "We consider", "We notice also", "Our goal is to", "In our work"
- **Logical connectors:** "Therefore", "Indeed", "Furthermore", "In fact", "Since", "Consequently"
- **Rhetorical questions** to open sections: "So what are...?", "What are the fundamentals of...?"
- **Announce structure** explicitly in the Introduction: "Following the introduction... Section 2... Finally Section 8"
- **Highlight originality:** "The originality of our work consists in..."
- **Confirm results:** "which confirms also...", "these results prove the effectiveness of..."
- **End with forward-looking statements:** "We hope that...", "We plan to extend this work..."
- Keep sentences **clear, direct, and technically precise**
- Mix passive and active voice for variety
- Justify every methodological choice explicitly

- **Inclusion criteria:**

  - Developing country context (or transferable findings)
  - Published 2018–2025
- **Exclusion criteria:**
  - Non-IoT studies
  - Purely theoretical without application
  - Duplicate publications
  - Studies not related to SDG context
