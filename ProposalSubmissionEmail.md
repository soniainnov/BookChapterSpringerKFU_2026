**Proposed Chapter Title:**
A Systematic Review of IoT-Enabled Smart Agriculture for Food Security and Sustainable Development: Opportunities, Challenges, and Future Directions

**Chapter Type:** Systematic Literature Review (PRISMA methodology)

**SDGs Addressed:** SDG 2 (Zero Hunger), SDG 9 (Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure), SDG 13 (Climate Action)

**Abstract:**

Food insecurity remains one of the most pressing challenges in developing countries, where smallholder farmers face compounding pressures from climate change, limited infrastructure, and restricted access to markets. The Internet of Things (IoT) has emerged as a transformative enabler of smart agriculture, offering real-time monitoring, precision irrigation, early pest detection, and climate-adaptive farming solutions that directly advance the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

This chapter presents a systematic review of the literature published between 2018 and 2025, following the PRISMA protocol, drawing from IEEE Xplore, Scopus, Web of Science, Google Scholar, and the ACM Digital Library. We examine both the positive impacts of IoT in smart agriculture — including water efficiency, crop yield optimization, and improved market access for smallholders — and its negative consequences, such as e-waste generation, energy consumption of sensor infrastructure, cybersecurity vulnerabilities, and the digital divide that risks marginalizing resource-constrained farmers.

We further analyze the specific deployment challenges in developing countries, including connectivity gaps, high hardware costs, limited digital literacy, and interoperability issues. The chapter concludes with forward-looking directions encompassing energy-harvesting IoT devices, AI and edge computing integration, blockchain-based supply chain traceability, and federated learning for privacy-preserving agricultural data sharing. Policy recommendations are provided to guide governments, international organizations, and technology developers toward inclusive and sustainable digital agriculture.
